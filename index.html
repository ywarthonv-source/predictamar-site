The challenge

The ocean is dynamic.
Governance is not.

Fisheries authorities and regulatory bodies make decisions based on data that is months or years old. Meanwhile, the ocean changes daily — thermal fronts shift, upwelling zones collapse, biomass migrates.

The gap between oceanographic reality and regulatory response is costing food security, accelerating overexploitation, and leaving coastal communities without the intelligence they need to adapt.

PredictaMAR closes that gap with near-real-time operational intelligence derived from open satellite data.

Architecture

Multi-scale ocean intelligence

PredictaMAR fuses data from three satellite scales into a single operational score — updated automatically four times per day and delivered through a lightweight decision interface.

01
Macro scale
Regional oceanographic context
Chlorophyll-a, sea surface temperature, sea level anomaly, ocean currents and thermal fronts across the full coastal domain — updated every 6 hours.
CMEMS OLCI 300m SST 0.083° SLA altimetry VIIRS NASA 4km
02
Tactical scale
Filament and front detection
Biological productivity gradients, thermal discontinuities and coastal upwelling structures detected at 300m resolution — identifying the boundaries where biomass concentrates.
Sentinel-3 OLCI Ic_v2 stability Gradient analysis
03
Coastal refinement
20-metre nearshore intelligence
Floating Algae Index and NIR/SWIR biological ratio from Sentinel-2 refine the score in nearshore zones — highest resolution layer available from open satellite data.
Sentinel-2 GEE FAI index ISB ratio 20m resolution
